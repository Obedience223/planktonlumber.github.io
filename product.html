Plywood
+ −

Plywood is a versatile type of lumber composed of thin layers of wood veneer glued together. It is known for its strength, stability, and resistance to warping. Plywood is used in various applications, including:

  • Construction: Plywood is commonly used for sheathing, subfloors, roofing, and structural components in residential and commercial buildings.
  • Furniture: It serves as a material for cabinets, shelves, and other furniture pieces.
  • Cabinetry: Plywood is utilized for making cabinet boxes and shelving.
  • Craft and DIY Projects: It is popular for creating home décor items, storage solutions, and other craft projects.

We offer high-quality plywood that is perfect for sheathing, subfloors, and structural applications. Our plywood products provide exceptional strength, stability, and durability, making them an excellent choice for both residential and commercial projects.

Softwood
+ −

Softwood comes from coniferous trees, which are evergreen and have needle-like leaves. Examples of softwood species include pine, spruce, cedar, and fir. Softwood is widely used in:

  • Construction: Softwood lumber is extensively used for framing, roofing, decking, and general construction purposes.
  • Outdoor Structures: It is commonly used for outdoor projects like fences, decks, pergolas, and garden furniture.
  • Cabinetry and Millwork: Softwood is employed for making cabinets, shelves, and interior millwork components.
  • Pallets and Packaging: Softwood lumber is used for manufacturing pallets, crates, and packaging materials.

We provide a wide range of softwood options, such as pine, spruce, cedar, and fir. Softwood lumber is popular for its versatility and affordability, making it suitable for a variety of applications, including framing, decking, fencing, and exterior siding.

Hardwood
+ −

Hardwood lumber comes from deciduous trees that shed their leaves annually. Some popular hardwood species include oak, maple, cherry, walnut, and mahogany. Hardwood is valued for its strength, durability, and attractive appearance. It finds applications in:

  • Flooring: Hardwood flooring is a popular choice for residential and commercial spaces due to its natural beauty and long-lasting qualities.
  • Cabinetry and Furniture: Hardwood is often used to create high-quality cabinets, tables, chairs, and other furniture pieces.
  • Interior Trim: It is used for moldings, baseboards, door frames, and window casings to add elegance and detail to interior spaces.
  • Musical Instruments: Many musical instruments, such as guitars and pianos, are crafted from hardwoods for their tonal qualities and aesthetics.

Our hardwood selection features an array of species, including oak, maple, cherry, walnut, and mahogany. These premium hardwoods are renowned for their beauty, strength, and durability. They are ideal for creating stunning furniture, cabinetry, flooring, and decorative architectural elements.

Redwood
+ −

Redwood is a type of softwood known for its natural beauty, durability, and resistance to decay and insects. It is primarily used for outdoor applications due to its natural resistance to rot and weathering. Common uses of redwood include:

Composite Lumber
+ −

Composite lumber is made from a combination of wood fibers or sawdust and plastic or resin binders. It is a durable and low-maintenance alternative to traditional lumber. Some common uses of composite lumber include:

  • Decking: Composite decking boards are popular for their resistance to rot, insects, and fading. They require minimal upkeep and are available in various colors and textures.
  • Outdoor Furniture: Composite lumber is often used for crafting outdoor furniture pieces like benches, tables, and chairs.
  • Landscaping: It can be used for creating garden borders, raised beds, and other landscaping features.

Our composite lumber products are engineered for durability and low maintenance. They are made from a combination of wood fibers or sawdust and plastic or resin binders. Composite lumber is an excellent choice for decking, outdoor furniture, and other applications requiring long-lasting, weather-resistant materials.

Engineering wood product
+ −

Engineered wood products are manufactured by binding together wood strands, fibers, or veneers using adhesives and heat. They offer enhanced strength, stability, and dimensional consistency. Examples of engineered wood products include:

  • Engineered Lumber: Products like laminated veneer lumber (LVL) and I-joists are used for structural purposes, such as beams, headers, and floor joists.
  • Engineered Flooring: Engineered wood flooring consists of a real wood veneer layer bonded to plywood or high-density fiberboard (HDF) for stability and durability.
  • Engineered Siding: Engineered wood siding offers the look of natural wood with improved durability and resistance to moisture and pests.

We offer a selection of engineered wood products, including laminated veneer lumber (LVL), I-joists, and engineered flooring. These products provide superior strength, stability, and dimensional consistency, making them ideal for structural applications and flooring installations.

Cedar
+ −

Cedar lumber is known for its natural resistance to decay, insects, and weathering. It is a versatile material used for various purposes, including:

  • Outdoor Structures: Cedar is popular for building decks, pergolas, and gazebos due to its durability and aesthetic appeal.
  • Siding and Cladding: It is used for exterior siding, shingles, and trim, providing a beautiful and long-lasting finish.
  • Interior Paneling: Cedar paneling is used to create warm and rustic interior walls and ceilings.
  • Closet and Storage Systems: Cedar is often used for making closet organizers, chests, and storage boxes due to its natural moth-repellent properties.

Cedar lumber is renowned for its natural beauty, durability, and resistance to decay and insects. It is commonly used for outdoor projects, including decking, siding, fencing, and landscaping features. Cedar products provide a timeless and elegant look to any exterior application.

Exotic Harwood
+ −

Exotic hardwoods are known for their unique grain patterns, colors, and durability. They are highly sought after for their aesthetic appeal and strength. Some examples include:

  • Teak: Teak lumber is renowned for its natural oils and high resistance to moisture, making it suitable for outdoor furniture, boat building, and decking.

  • Ebony: Ebony is a dense, dark wood used for intricate woodwork, musical instrument fingerboards, and luxury furniture pieces.

Our collection of exotic hardwoods includes species like teak, mahogany, ebony, and more. These unique woods offer exceptional beauty, distinctive grain patterns, and superior strength. Exotic hardwoods are often used for high-end furniture, cabinetry, musical instruments, and specialty woodworking projects.
